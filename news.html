Date Line: 03/29/2026

The Week That Was

A week of fresh faces, fresh bruises, and one very productive trip to the cash cage by NASA Al.

The TLPT site tells you exactly what kind of circus this is: The League. The Players. The Tilt. The homepage promises that the stats never forget, the standings page lets you sort the damage every which way, the dashboard turns the whole thing into a public audit, and the honors page exists mostly to embarrass people with formatting. In other words, this league did not merely have a week. It left evidence.

🎯 The Big Story

The biggest story was not at the top of the profit board, where Li-Fo still sits. It was one rung below, where NASA Al decided subtlety was overrated. He went from $290 to $425 in profit, from 11 hits to 15, and from 6 cashes to 7. That pushed him from third to second in profit and from third to second in luck. A nice, tidy little heater — the kind that doesn’t scream for attention because it’s too busy cashing.

Meanwhile, the loudest entrance belonged to Mike-G, who had previously contributed exactly nothing to the numbers except blank space. Now? Two entries, five hits, one cash, $105 profit, and a monster jump onto the board. That’s not “getting acclimated.” That’s showing up late and acting like you own the building.

And then there was ProvidenceMike, who volunteered as this week’s cautionary tale. Four more entries, one more hit, zero new cashes, and profit sliding from -$90 to -$210. The note in his player file says, “I definitely should not be in this hand.” At least one part of the dataset remains brutally honest.

📈 Movers

Risers —

  • NASA Al: the cleanest established riser on the board. More profit, more hits, more cashes, better ROI, less tilt. He didn’t just have a good week; he had the sort of week that makes other people mutter about seat draws and deck temperature.
  • Mike-G: shot from brand-new face to instant factor. He debuted with real juice: profit, hits, a cash, and the kind of rate stats that make veterans immediately start explaining sample size. That is a debut and a threat letter in the same envelope.
  • Pat: didn’t play much, but he made it count. One entry, one cash, $70 profit, and a league-best 233.3% ROI, which now owns the record previously held by Big Tim. Small sample? Of course. Still counts? Also of course.
  • Kishan: also arrived with intent: one entry, one cash, two hits, $35 profit, and a very efficient first week. It’s hard to ask for a more effective hello.
  • Red: somehow managed a weirdly TLPT week: profit got a little worse, but the mayhem improved. He added five hits, one cash, and climbed the hits board. A man can lose money and still leave fingerprints all over the furniture.

Fallers —

  • ProvidenceMike: took the hardest punch. Four more entries and a $120 loss swing is the kind of thing that makes the spreadsheet look like it wants to call a wellness check.
  • Li-Fo: remained the profit leader, but the week still nicked the shine. Profit fell from $555 to $495, ROI sagged from 68.5% to 56.9%, and the records board no longer shows his name next to most cashes. Still rich, just slightly less smug.
  • Hiro: had one of those weeks where the prime-number outs apparently remained theoretical. Two more entries, no new hits, no new cashes, and profit dropping from -$105 to -$165. The math may still be beautiful. The results were not.
  • Ahmed: added a bubble and lost $30 in profit. That is a very particular brand of annoyance — active enough to feel involved, punished enough to regret it.
  • BostnMike: had the classic commissioner’s paradox: more hits, more cashes, more bubbles, less actual joy. He now owns Most Hits and Most Cashes, but the weekly line still came with a profit drop and a slide in ROI and Power. The counting stats say “workhorse.” The efficiency stats say “maybe sit down for a minute.”

🔢 Numbers That Matter

  • +$135: NASA Al’s profit swing, the best among returning regulars. Quietly the strongest real heater of the week.
  • Mike-G’s immediate spike: going from nonexistent in the weekly story to instantly relevant is about as subtle as flipping the table and then apologizing for the noise.
  • 233.3% ROI: Pat now holds the best ROI record, replacing Big Tim’s 183.3%. Nothing says “welcome to the spreadsheet” like taking a record before people remember your seating preference.
  • BostnMike: 26 to 28 hits, 11 to 12 cashes, 5 to 6 bubbles. That’s the full TLPT experience right there: violence, profit, and emotional damage, all in one row.
  • ProvidenceMike: -$90 to -$210. Same five cashes, four extra entries, and all the romance of walking into a rake trap.
  • Nitro: remains the basement tenant on the profit board, sliding from -$270 to -$300, and still living in worst-luck territory. At some point the variance speech starts sounding like performance art.

🎉 Welcome to the League

A proper welcome is in order, because Mike-G, Pat, and Kishan are not just new names in the file — they arrived like men who had heard the league was accepting applications for immediate chaos.

Mike-G did not tiptoe in. He kicked the saloon doors open. Two entries, five hits, one cash, and a triple-digit profit debut is the kind of opening week that makes established players suddenly become historians of “long-term sustainability.” He didn’t just join the madness. He added punctuation.

Pat took the more elegant route: one appearance, one cash, one tidy $70 profit, and then casually walked off with the best ROI mark on the board. That’s a wonderfully rude thing to do in your first week. Some guys join a league to get their bearings. Pat apparently joined to steal shelf space on the honors page.

Kishan also understood the assignment. One entry, one cash, two hits, profit in the black, and no wasted motion. It was a clean, efficient debut — the statistical equivalent of showing up to a beer league and immediately having the best wrist shot in warmups.

Together, the three newcomers gave the week its best subplot: fresh blood arriving and instantly making the regulars uncomfortable. Which, frankly, is exactly what you want in a league like this. Welcome to TLPT — where the banter is loud, the metrics are suspiciously thorough, and the spreadsheet will remember everything.

🔥 Quick Hits

  • Mike-G spent one week in the league and already has more momentum than half the room.
  • Pat took the ROI record after one entry, which is either elite efficiency or the funniest possible use of a sample size.
  • Kishan posted two hits in one entry. Some people ease in. He apparently chose violence.
  • NASA Al looked like the only man at the table who filed a flight plan.
  • ProvidenceMike added four entries and no cashes. That’s not a grind. That’s a subscription.
  • BostnMike broke records and still managed to make the week feel expensive. A true league ambassador.
  • Li-Fo is still the profit boss, just with a little less polish and a few more tire marks.
  • Red lost money but climbed the hits board, which feels very on brand for a man apparently trying to settle hands and old grudges at once.
  • Hiro’s results suggest the prime number of outs may have been zero.
  • Nitro remains faithful to the low-profit arts.

🎬 Closing

That was TLPT in a nutshell: a veteran heater, a few statistical body blows, a burst of noisy new blood, and just enough movement on the board to keep everyone checking the site like it owes them an apology. The stats never forget. This week, they also didn’t forgive.
